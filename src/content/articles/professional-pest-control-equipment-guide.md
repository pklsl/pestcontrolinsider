Professional-grade pest control equipment is available to homeowners — and for serious infestations or large properties, it makes a significant difference in results. Understanding what equipment professionals use and why helps you make better purchasing decisions, whether you're outfitting a new pest control business or tackling a persistent problem at home.

This guide covers the key categories of pest control equipment: sprayers, dusters, rodent control hardware, termite equipment, and PPE.

## Sprayers

The sprayer is the primary tool for applying liquid pesticides — exterior perimeter treatments, crack-and-crevice applications, and contact kill sprays.

### Hand-Pump Sprayers (1–2 Gallon)

Hand-pump sprayers are the workhorse of indoor pest control. They allow precise, low-volume applications in cracks, crevices, under appliances, and along baseboards.

**B&G 1-Gallon Sprayer (Model 1150)** — The standard of the professional pest control industry. Stainless steel wand, Viton seals (chemical-resistant), adjustable fan and cone patterns, and a pressure release valve. Built to last decades with minimal maintenance.

- **Best for:** Indoor crack-and-crevice; perimeter application along baseboards; targeted spot treatments
- **Price:** $120–$150 (Amazon affiliate: pestcontrolins-20)

**Chapin 1949 1-Gallon Poly Sprayer** — A less expensive poly sprayer suitable for occasional use. Not as durable as the B&G, but adequate for homeowner use.

- **Price:** $20–$35

### Backpack Sprayers (4 Gallon)

Backpack sprayers carry 4 gallons of mixed solution, allowing exterior perimeter treatment of a home without refilling. Essential for any outdoor application program.

**Solo 475-B 4-Gallon Professional Piston Backpack Sprayer** — A top choice among pest control professionals and serious DIYers. Piston pump provides consistent pressure; comfortable padded shoulder straps; 14-inch wand with adjustable nozzle.

- **Best for:** Exterior perimeter treatments; lawn applications; large-scale residual sprays
- **Price:** $150–$200 (Amazon affiliate: pestcontrolins-20)

**Chapin 63985 4-Gallon Backpack Sprayer** — Commercial-grade construction with a longer pumping stroke for faster pressurization. Poly tank with stainless steel wand.

- **Price:** $140–$180

**Field King 190328 4-Gallon Professional Backpack Sprayer** — Popular with homeowners for its no-leak pump design and more comfortable harness.

- **Price:** $80–$100 — excellent value for homeowner-level use

**Key backpack sprayer features to look for:**
- **Viton or chemical-resistant seals** — standard rubber degrades with pesticide contact
- **Adjustable pressure** — different applications require different spray patterns
- **Stainless steel wand** — more durable than plastic
- **Padded straps** — comfort matters during a 2-hour exterior treatment

### Power Sprayers / Skid Sprayers

Professional pest control trucks are often equipped with skid-mounted power sprayers — electric or gas-powered pumps connected to 25- to 50-gallon tanks, with 100+ feet of hose on a reel. These are overkill for homeowners but standard for companies doing frequent large-property treatments.

**Southland SWP43E Electric Power Sprayer** — An entry-level skid sprayer suitable for small operations or homeowners with large acreage.

- **Price:** $200–$350

### Hose-End Sprayers

Hose-end sprayers attach to a garden hose and meter concentrate from a bottle into the water stream. They're less precise than pump sprayers but easy to use for large lawn treatments.

**Chapin G390 Hose-End Sprayer** — Adjustable dilution ratio (1 oz to 16 oz per gallon). Works well for Bifen IT exterior concentrate applications.

- **Price:** $20–$30

## Dusters

Dusters apply insecticide dust (boric acid, CimeXa, diatomaceous earth, Delta Dust) into wall voids, electrical outlets, attics, crawl spaces, and other areas where liquid sprays can't reach or aren't appropriate.

### Bellows Dusters

**KNESS Scatterbox Duster** — A classic metal bellows duster with a flexible rubber tip. Precise dust application into cracks, outlet boxes, and conduit penetrations.

- **Price:** $30–$40

**B&G Bulb Duster (Model 1150)** — The professional standard. Brass construction, precise control, and appropriate for all insecticide dusts.

- **Price:** $40–$60

**DIY Tip:** Dusters work best when they're filled no more than half full — a too-full duster clogs or produces uneven application. Tap the duster before use to loosen clumped dust.

### Hand-Pump Dusters

For larger-volume dust applications (attics, crawl spaces, wall voids via drilling), a hand-pump duster with an extension wand provides more reach.

**Bello Hand Pump Duster** — 1-quart capacity with a 24-inch flexible hose extension. Good for crawl space and attic applications.

- **Price:** $40–$60

### Electric Dusters

Battery-powered electric dusters are used for large crawl space treatments or when operator fatigue is a concern in commercial applications.

**Dustick Insecticide Duster** — Extends up to 6 feet; useful for hard-to-reach areas.

- **Price:** $200–$350

## Rodent Control Equipment

### Snap Traps

**Victor Professional Snap Traps (M326)** — The wooden snap trap that has defined rodent control for over a century. Effective, inexpensive, and reusable. Buy in bulk: 72-packs from professional suppliers.

- **Price:** $30–$40 for a 12-pack on Amazon (pestcontrolins-20)

**T-Rex Trap by Catchmaster** — A plastic enclosed snap trap designed for professional use in rodent bait stations. Can be set with one hand; no exposed springs.

- **Price:** $25–$35 for a 6-pack

### Bait Stations

**Protecta LP Rat Station** — The professional standard for exterior rat bait stations. Tamper-resistant, lockable, and accepts rodenticide block or place packs. Required by most pest control standards for any exterior rodenticide application (to protect children, pets, and non-target wildlife).

- **Price:** $15–$25 each; buy 6–12 for a perimeter program

**Trapper T-Rex Mouse Station** — Smaller station for interior mouse control. Holds two snap traps hidden from view — important for accounts with children or pets.

- **Price:** $20–$35 for a 2-pack

### Monitoring Stations

**Tin Cat Multi-Catch Mouse Trap** — A no-kill multi-catch trap. Primarily used for monitoring — the number of mice caught indicates population size. Useful for commercial accounts and ongoing monitoring programs.

- **Price:** $15–$25

### Exclusion Materials

**Xcluder Fill Fabric** — Stainless steel and poly fiber fill material that rodents can't chew through. Stuffed into gaps around pipes, conduit, and utility penetrations.

- **Price:** $40–$70 for a roll (Amazon affiliate: pestcontrolins-20)

**1/4-inch Hardware Cloth** — Galvanized steel mesh for sealing larger openings. Essential for rodent exclusion work.

## Termite Equipment

Termite treatment is a specialized service requiring additional equipment beyond general pest control.

### Liquid Termiticide Application

**Soil Rod and Slide Hammer** — Used to inject termiticide (Termidor, Taurus SC) into the soil along the foundation. Professional treatment requires injecting at 12-inch intervals, 4–6 inches from the foundation.

- **Price:** $80–$200 for a rod-and-hammer kit

**Hammer Drill with Masonry Bit** — For drilling through concrete slabs, driveways, or concrete patios to access soil underneath. Standard professional equipment.

### Termite Inspection Tools

**Moisture Meter** — Termites (and wood rot) are attracted to moisture. A moisture meter reads percent wood moisture — readings above 20% indicate elevated risk.

- **Ryobi Moisture Meter (TMM200)** — $30–$40; adequate for inspection work.
- **Tramex Encounter Plus** — $300–$500; professional standard, reads through flooring without contact

**Termatrac T3i** — A specialized motion-detection device that detects termite movement within walls without drilling. Used by professional inspectors. Cost: $3,000+.

**Inspection Mirror and Flashlight** — Basic but essential. Use a flex-head inspection mirror to see under sill plates and into crawl space framing.

## Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

PPE is not optional — pesticides require appropriate protective equipment as specified on each product label. **The label is the law.**

### Respiratory Protection

**N95 Disposable Respirator** — Minimum for dust applications (boric acid, diatomaceous earth, CimeXa). Does not protect against organic vapors from spray applications.

**3M 6500 Half-Face Respirator with OV/P100 Cartridges** — The professional standard for spray applications. Filters both particulates and organic vapors.

- **Price:** $35–$50 for the respirator; $20–$30 for cartridges

**Full-Face Respirator** — Required for fumigation and certain high-exposure applications. Not typically needed for general pest control.

### Gloves

**Chemical-resistant nitrile gloves** — Buy in bulk. Disposable, but more resistant to pesticide permeation than latex.

**Chemical-resistant rubber gloves** — For handling concentrate mixing; heavy-duty.

### Protective Clothing

**Tyvek disposable coveralls** — Used for high-exposure applications (attic treatments, crawl space work). Keep a box in the truck.

**Chemical-resistant apron** — For mixing concentrates at the truck.

**Safety glasses / chemical splash goggles** — Required for mixing and application with most pesticide labels.

### Boots

**Chemical-resistant rubber boots** — Required for applications involving significant soil contact or wading through treated areas. Standard for termite soil treatments.

## Application Accessories

### Nozzle Tips

Spray tip selection determines spray pattern and droplet size:

- **Flat fan tip** — For broadcast spray; most exterior perimeter work
- **Cone tip** — For misting and more even coverage on plant surfaces
- **Stream tip** — For directed crack-and-crevice applications

TeeJet spray tips are the professional standard and available from pest control suppliers.

### Mixing Equipment

**Graduated cylinder (100 mL)** — For accurate concentrate measurement
**Chemical-resistant mixing container** — For pre-mixing concentrates before adding to sprayer
**Calibration cups** — For calibrating sprayer output rate

### Spray Markers

**Rockit Spray Marker Dye** — Blue dye added to spray mix to show where you've sprayed. Useful for large exterior applications to avoid gaps or overlaps.

## Bottom Line

The right equipment makes pest control more effective and safer. A professional-grade sprayer applies chemicals more accurately than a consumer garden sprayer, a proper duster gets insecticide into wall voids where pests harbor, and appropriate PPE protects you from the chemicals you're working with. Whether you're a homeowner dealing with a serious infestation or a technician starting in the industry, invest in quality tools — they pay for themselves in results and reduced chemical waste.
