Spring is the most important time of year for pest prevention. As temperatures rise above 50°F, insect activity resurges, queen wasps emerge to establish new colonies, termite swarms begin, and overwintering mice become active again. A few hours of preventive work in early spring dramatically reduces pest pressure throughout the entire year.

This 12-step checklist covers everything from exterior inspection to product application, organized from most to least impactful.

## Step 1: Inspect and Clean Gutters

**Target pests:** Mosquitoes, carpenter ants, termites, hornets

Clogged gutters hold standing water — a breeding site for mosquitoes — and retain moisture that damages fascia boards, attracting carpenter ants and termites. Remove winter debris, check for damaged sections, and confirm water drains away from the foundation through functional downspouts.

**Product recommendation:** Install gutter guards (Amerimax Home Products Mesh Gutter Guard) to minimize future clogging.

## Step 2: Seal Cracks and Gaps in the Exterior

**Target pests:** Mice, ants, roaches, silverfish, all indoor-invading pests

Walk the entire exterior of your home with a caulk gun. Focus on:
- Window and door frames
- Gaps around utility penetrations (electrical, plumbing, cable)
- Foundation cracks
- Gaps where siding meets the foundation
- Around the chimney and roof penetrations

**Product recommendation:** DAP Alex Plus Acrylic Latex Caulk (exterior grade) for most sealing; Great Stuff Gaps and Cracks Foam for larger voids.

## Step 3: Check and Replace Door Sweeps and Weatherstripping

**Target pests:** Mice, ants, crickets, silverfish

A gap under a door of just 1/4 inch is large enough for a mouse to squeeze through. Replace worn door sweeps and weatherstripping on all exterior doors, including the garage door seal.

**Product recommendation:** M-D Building Products Heavy Duty Door Sweep (stainless steel base that mice can't chew through).

## Step 4: Apply Perimeter Spray Around the Foundation

**Target pests:** Ants, roaches, spiders, earwigs, crickets, centipedes

A bifenthrin or cypermethrin-based perimeter spray applied around the foundation creates a barrier that kills insects attempting to enter. Apply to:
- The foundation wall (3 feet up)
- The soil or mulch against the foundation (3 feet out)
- Around garage door perimeter
- Around window wells

**Product recommendation:** Ortho Home Defense Max (ready-to-use) or Bifen IT concentrate (more economical, requires a pump sprayer).

**Timing:** Apply when temperatures are above 50°F and no rain is expected for 24 hours. Reapply every 90 days through summer.

## Step 5: Treat the Lawn for Grubs (Early Spring)

**Target pests:** Japanese beetles (adults in summer), grubs (larvae in soil), moles (attracted by grubs)

Apply a preventive grub control product in early spring before adult beetles emerge and lay eggs.

**Product recommendation:** Scotts GrubEx (chlorantraniliprole) — apply in April–May for prevention; lasts through the season. SaferGro Grub Killer (for organic approach — beneficial nematodes applied to moist soil).

## Step 6: Inspect and Treat Crawl Space

**Target pests:** Silverfish, centipedes, subterranean termites, rodents

Check the crawl space for:
- Moisture issues and standing water
- Wood debris that should be removed
- Mud tubes on foundation walls (termite sign)
- Damaged vapor barrier

Actions to take:
- Remove any wood debris or cardboard from the crawl space
- Repair or replace the vapor barrier if damaged
- Apply diatomaceous earth along the perimeter
- Contact a pest professional if you find mud tubes

**Product recommendation:** DampRid Moisture Absorber for localized moisture areas; Humidex Crawl Space Dehumidifier for significant moisture.

## Step 7: Check for and Remove Wasp Nesting Sites

**Target pests:** Paper wasps, yellowjackets, hornets

Walk the exterior and check:
- Under eaves and porch ceilings
- Inside outdoor furniture (umbrella frames, chair cushion housings)
- Inside the mailbox, BBQ cover, and any sheltered outdoor structures
- Around the grill and outdoor appliances

Remove abandoned nests from last season — some wasps reuse old nest sites. Seal gaps and crevices where queens might establish new nests.

**Product recommendation:** Caulk all gaps in eaves and soffits before queens establish in spring (April–May is the window).

## Step 8: Set Up Mosquito Prevention

**Target pests:** Mosquitoes

Tip, toss, or treat anything that can hold water:
- Empty saucers under planters
- Toss old tires or store them indoors
- Tip wheelbarrows and buckets
- Add a fountain pump to birdbaths and ornamental ponds
- Clean gutters (see Step 1)
- Apply Mosquito Dunks (BTI) to ponds, rain barrels, and drainage areas

Start the season with a yard bifenthrin spray on vegetation to knock down overwintered adults and provide a month of residual protection.

## Step 9: Inspect and Place Termite Monitoring

**Target pests:** Subterranean termites

Spring is termite swarmer season — the most visible sign of nearby colonies. Inspect the foundation for mud tubes during your exterior walk. Check the crawl space for active tubes on concrete piers and foundation walls.

If you have a professional termite service or bait system, ensure your annual inspection is scheduled.

**Product recommendation:** Spectracide Terminate Bait Sticks (for DIY monitoring around the foundation perimeter in low-risk areas).

## Step 10: Address Rodent Entry Points

**Target pests:** Mice, rats, squirrels

After winter, mice that were harboring outdoors begin actively foraging. Check for new gnaw marks around exterior doors and utility entry points. Recheck all gaps identified previously.

Set 2–4 snap traps in the garage, basement, and attic as a monitoring measure — catching mice early prevents breeding inside.

**Product recommendation:** Victor Original Mouse Snap Traps (multi-pack); Xcluder Rodent Control Fill Fabric for pipe gaps.

## Step 11: Inspect and Treat for Carpenter Ants

**Target pests:** Carpenter ants

Spring is when carpenter ant parent colonies become active and satellite colonies emerge. Look for:
- Large black ants (1/4 to 3/4 inch) emerging from walls, window frames, or wood structures
- Small piles of sawdust-like frass near wooden structural elements
- Winged carpenter ant swarmers inside the home

If you find evidence, apply gel bait (Advance Carpenter Ant Bait) near active trails and have the structure inspected for moisture damage.

## Step 12: Treat Outdoor Pest Hotspots

**Target pests:** Fleas, ticks, ants, fire ants

Treat outdoor areas most likely to harbor or produce pest pressure:

- **Shaded yard areas** (under decks, dense plantings): Apply bifenthrin or permethrin spray for flea, tick, and ant control
- **Lawn edges near woods**: Apply tick treatment spray (bifenthrin) along the "transition zone" where lawn meets wood cover
- **Fire ant mounds**: Apply Amdro broadcast bait before colonies grow to full size
- **Garden beds**: Apply DE around bed perimeters and set up beneficial insect habitat

## Bottom Line

Spring pest control is primarily about prevention — sealing entry points before pests get in, applying perimeter treatments before populations build, and removing harborage and water sources before they become breeding grounds. Two to three hours of preventive work in early spring reduces pest pressure for the entire season. Follow this checklist sequentially and re-inspect in June to assess how well prevention is working.
