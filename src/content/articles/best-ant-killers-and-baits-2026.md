Not all ant killers work on all ant species. The best ant bait for odorous house ants may be largely ignored by carpenter ants. Fire ant control requires a completely different approach than pavement ant treatment. Choosing the right product for the right ant is the single most important factor in successful control.

This guide reviews the top ant killers and baits in 2026, organized by ant species.

## Why Species-Matching Matters

Ant baits work by exploiting ants' food preferences — different species prefer different food sources at different times of year:

- **Sugar/carbohydrate baits** (e.g., borax with sweet liquid): Effective for odorous house ants, pavement ants, and Argentine ants
- **Protein/oil baits** (e.g., gel bait with protein attractant): More effective for grease ants, carpenter ants, and fire ants
- **Combination baits**: Effective across a broader range of species

Using a sugar-based bait on carpenter ants won't work — and vice versa. Correctly identifying your ant species before buying determines whether your treatment succeeds.

## Best for Odorous House Ants (Most Common Indoor Ant)

### Best Overall: TERRO T300B Liquid Ant Bait Stations

**The most popular and most effective bait for odorous house ants.**

TERRO T300B pre-filled stations contain borax dissolved in sweetened liquid — exactly what odorous house ants prefer. Workers carry the bait back to the colony, where it slowly kills the queen and other workers.

**Active ingredient:** Borax (disodium tetraborate)
**Form:** Pre-filled liquid bait stations (6 stations per pack)

**Pros:**
- Extremely effective for odorous house ants and pavement ants
- Pre-filled, no mixing required
- Ready to use out of the package
- Long-lasting stations (up to 3 months)
- Kills the colony, not just foragers

**Cons:**
- Not effective for all species — particularly less effective for carpenter ants and fire ants
- Takes 1–2 weeks to fully kill the colony (patience required)
- Must be kept away from other insecticides (repellent sprays deter ants from taking the bait)

**Best for:** Odorous house ants, pavement ants, little black ants

**Price:** $ (6-pack, approx. $8–$12)

## Best for Carpenter Ants

### Best Gel Bait: Advance Carpenter Ant Bait

Carpenter ants prefer protein-based food sources, particularly during spring and summer. Advance Carpenter Ant Bait contains a combination of sugar and protein attractants with imidacloprid as the active ingredient.

**Active ingredient:** Imidacloprid
**Form:** Granular bait

**Pros:**
- Specifically formulated for carpenter ant food preferences
- Effective for killing the parent colony when placed near satellite colony locations
- Can be used outdoors near stumps, woodpiles, and foundation areas

**Cons:**
- More expensive than liquid bait
- Takes longer to see results — carpenter ant colonies are smaller but more dispersed

**Best for:** Carpenter ants

**Price:** $$ (8 oz, approx. $25–$35)

### Supplement: Carpenter Ant Gel Bait

**Maxforce Carpenter Ant Gel Bait** (fipronil) is a professional-grade gel that can be applied in wall voids and in crevices where carpenter ants travel. Highly effective.

## Best for Fire Ants

### Best Broadcast Bait: Amdro Fire Ant Bait

Amdro is the gold standard for fire ant broadcast bait treatment — it's been used for decades and remains one of the most effective consumer fire ant products available.

**Active ingredient:** Hydramethylnon
**Form:** Granular bait

**Application:** Broadcast over infested lawn areas (or apply directly to individual mounds), following package directions. Workers carry the bait back to the mound and feed it to the queen.

**Pros:**
- Kills the queen — eliminates the colony, not just surface workers
- Can be broadcast over large areas
- Takes 1–4 weeks for full effect

**Cons:**
- Not waterproof — apply when no rain is expected for 24 hours
- Fresh bait is more attractive than old bait — store properly

**Price:** $$ (approx. $15–$25 for a 2 lb bag)

### Individual Mound Treatment: Ortho Fire Ant Killer Mound Treatment

For spot-treating individual mounds quickly. Apply directly to the mound and water in. Works faster than bait but doesn't spread through the colony as effectively.

## Best for Argentine Ants

Argentine ants (*Linepithema humile*) form "supercolonies" — interconnected colonies with multiple queens that can span entire city blocks. They're particularly difficult to control because killing one section of the colony merely relocates it.

### Best Bait: TERRO T300B (Sugar Bait)

The borax-based TERRO liquid bait is also highly effective for Argentine ants. The key is placing many stations throughout the colony's range — not just at the interior ant trails.

**Important:** Argentine ant control requires sustained bait application. Place stations along fence lines, around the foundation perimeter, and wherever trails are observed — indoors and out.

### Professional-Grade: Optigard Ant Gel Bait

**Optigard** (thiamethoxam) is a professional-grade gel bait with a sugar attractant that's highly effective for Argentine ants. It's available to consumers online and through pest supply retailers.

**Price:** $$$

## Best Perimeter Spray: Taurus SC / Termidor SC (Bifenthrin/Fipronil)

For perimeter control around the home — stopping ants before they enter — a professional-grade perimeter spray provides the longest residual protection.

**Bifen IT** (bifenthrin concentrate) is the most widely used professional perimeter spray available to consumers. Diluted and applied around the foundation, it provides 30–90 day residual ant control.

**Taurus SC** (fipronil) is the professional-grade equivalent that uses the same active ingredient as Termidor and is available online.

**Price:** $$$ (concentrate, approx. $30–$50 for multiple applications)

## Critical Rules for Bait Success

1. **Never spray insecticide near bait stations** — repellent residue causes ants to avoid the bait
2. **Use enough bait** — one or two stations is rarely enough; use 6–8 stations for a significant infestation
3. **Be patient** — bait takes 1–2 weeks to kill a colony; don't assume it's not working if ants are still present after 3 days
4. **Replace bait when it dries out or is depleted**
5. **Identify your species first** — the wrong bait wastes time and money

## Bottom Line

**TERRO T300B** is the single best ant product for most homeowners dealing with the most common household ant species (odorous house ants, pavement ants, Argentine ants). For carpenter ants, switch to **Advance Carpenter Ant Bait**. For fire ants, **Amdro** broadcast bait kills colonies. Supplement any bait program with a perimeter spray (Bifen IT or Ortho Home Defense) to create a barrier at entry points. Identify your ant species, match the product to the species, and be patient — colony elimination takes 1–2 weeks with the right bait.
